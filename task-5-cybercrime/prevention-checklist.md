# Stay Safe Online: Essential Student Checklist

As a college student in India, your digital life is likely centered around UPI payments, Aadhaar-linked services, and academic portals. While these tools make life easier, they also create specific vulnerabilities. Use this checklist to stay secure.

---

## 🛡️ Prevention Tips

1.  **The Golden Rule of UPI:** Never enter your UPI PIN to **receive** money. Scammers often send "Collect Requests" on apps like GPay or PhonePe, claiming you’ve won a scholarship or a prize. If a transaction asks for your PIN, money is **leaving** your account.
2.  **Avoid QR Code Traps:** Do not scan a QR code sent over WhatsApp or Telegram to "verify" your identity or "receive a payment." Legitimate companies and government portals will never ask you to scan a code to credit your account.
3.  **Use Masked Aadhaar:** When providing ID for hostel registrations, exams, or SIM cards, download a **Masked Aadhaar** from the UIDAI portal. This hides the first eight digits of your Aadhaar number, preventing your primary identity key from being harvested by low-security databases.
4.  **Lock Your Biometrics:** Use the **mAadhaar app** to "Lock" your biometrics. This prevents anyone from using your fingerprint data for unauthorized Aadhaar-enabled payment systems (AePS) or SIM swaps.
5.  **Identify 'Task-Based' Job Scams:** Be skeptical of WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering "Work from Home" or "YouTube Like" tasks. If a job requires you to pay a "security deposit" or "investment fee" to withdraw your earnings, it is a scam.
6.  **Secure Your WhatsApp:** Enable **Two-Step Verification** (Settings > Account). This adds a PIN requirement so that even if a scammer tricks you into sharing an OTP, they cannot log into your account on another device.
7.  **Audit Public Wi-Fi Use:** Avoid accessing your University LMS or banking apps over free public Wi-Fi in canteens or railway stations. If you must use it, always use a **Virtual Private Network (VPN)** to encrypt your data.
8.  **Check the 'Sender' Domain:** Phishing emails often look official but come from slightly misspelled domains (e.g., `support@g00gle.com` instead of `google.com`). Always tap the sender's name to see the actual email address before clicking any links.

## 🚨 Emergency Reporting Channels

If you fall victim to financial fraud or cybercrime, every minute counts. Use these official channels immediately:

> **National Cyber Crime Helpline:** Call **1930** > **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)

*Note: Reporting within the **"Golden Hour"** (the first 2 hours) is critical for freezing stolen funds before they are moved out of the banking system.*
